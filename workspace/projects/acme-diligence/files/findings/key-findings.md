# Key findings — Acme acquisition diligence

1. **Revenue quality:** ~60% of the target's monitoring revenue rode on reimbursement codes exposed to threshold/audit tightening. Stress-tested, the base case only held if two of three sensitivities stayed favourable.
2. **Cost-to-serve:** the target under-staffed alert triage; the "profitable" unit economics assumed an alert-handling efficiency they had never actually hit at scale.
3. **Retention:** monitored-patient retention was strong where RPM was paired with care coordination, weak where it was sold as a standalone convenience product.
4. **Decision dynamics:** Acme's leadership deferred entirely to the CFO's finance review. Aligning finance early — leading with the downside — was what unlocked the commitment.
