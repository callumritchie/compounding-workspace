# Recommendation — Harbor omnichannel

Use the convenience tier to **acquire**, not to defend margin. Willingness-to-pay for convenience is real but fragile — it erodes fast once a lower-friction rival appears. **Convenience is a wedge, not a moat.**

Durable margin comes from **cost-to-serve discipline** (fulfilment cost per order), the same pattern we've seen in healthcare RPM and in payments. Build the margin case on cost-to-serve, and stress-test the convenience-premium erosion explicitly.
