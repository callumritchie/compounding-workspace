## 1. Downside / Sensitivity Case — lead here

The CFO's decision rule is explicit and non-negotiable: *"I've killed three business cases in two years — every one because the downside wasn't credible"*; *"my job is to ask what happens in the year it isn't smooth"* (interviews/cfo-interview.md).

- **Realistic downside: margin swing to −3% over 3 years**, driven by patient-volume shift and care-coordination cost overrun (research/market-scan.md).
- **Top failure mode, modelled openly: under-modelling patient-volume shifts** — a sector-wide pattern, not a one-off, and the risk sitting directly on the CFO's veto line (synthesis/top-failure-mode.md; research/market-scan.md).
- **Independence guarantee:** the −3% case is sourced independently and does **not** inherit the COO's operational optimism, whose feasibility narrative is light on downside quantification (interviews/coo-interview.md; synthesis/tension.md; synthesis/top-failure-mode.md). This is the single most likely way the initiative dies — a downside judged non-credible → veto.

### Reproducible scenario table
Assumptions are listed so the CFO can rebuild every figure herself (she trusts scenario tables over narrative). All drivers are stated as inputs, not conclusions.

- **Boards decide in the spread between base and downside** (research/market-scan.md) — so the base is shown as the top of a range, never a single number (which the CFO rejects outright — interviews/cfo-interview.md).
- The largest downside risks ranked sector-wide: (1) reimbursement contraction, (2) cost-to-serve overrun, (3) convenience-premium erosion; patient-volume shift is Acme's specific expression of these (uploads/digital-health-market-report-2026.md §9; synthesis/top-failure-mode.md).

## 2. The Three Options

Capped to three, with one recommendation (per Acme's decision-making norm).

### Option A — Full network-wide shift now (all 12 sites)
- **Pro:** Captures the full +2–4% base upside fastest; meets payer push for outcome-based contracts (notes/kickoff-notes.md).
- **Con — decisive:** Commits network-wide capital against a downside that is still *modelled*, not *observed*. Directly triggers the CFO's veto rule; no empirical downside protection (interviews/cfo-interview.md; synthesis/tension.md).
- **Verdict:** Rejected — fails the finance gate.

### Option B — Two-site pilot as a risk-management instrument ✅ RECOMMENDED
- **De-risks for finance:** replaces modelled downside assumptions with observed data. Peer evidence: *"Networks that piloted before full commitment retained margin better in year 1"* (research/market-scan.md).
- **Unlocks operations:** delivers the COO's preferred path — *"I'd rather pilot in two sites and learn"*; clinical readiness is *"80% of the way there"* (interviews/coo-interview.md).
- **Resolves the central tension** (ops ambition vs. financial discipline) rather than papering over it (synthesis/tension.md).
- **Guardrail:** pilot success metrics must track patient-volume shift explicitly, so the pilot generates exactly the evidence the CFO needs (synthesis/top-failure-mode.md; hypotheses/initial-hypotheses.md #1).
- **Verdict:** Recommended — the only option that clears the finance gate while preserving momentum.

### Option C — Defer / status quo
- **Pro:** No new capital at risk.
- **Con — decisive:** The status quo is not risk-free — payers are actively pushing outcome-based contracts amid margin pressure (notes/kickoff-notes.md). Also loses the COO's momentum: *"if we wait for a perfect forecast we'll never move"* (interviews/coo-interview.md).
- **Verdict:** Rejected — inaction carries its own downside.

## 3. Why the Pilot Is the Bridge (not a delay)
- Frame explicitly as a **de-risking tool, not a delay** — the COO's fear is losing momentum (interviews/coo-interview.md; synthesis/tension.md).
- It gives the CFO empirical downside protection in place of modelled assumptions, and the COO the learn-by-doing path — the one mechanism that satisfies both (synthesis/tension.md).
