## Executive Summary
**Recommendation: Approve a two-site value-based care pilot, positioned as a risk-management instrument — not a full commitment and not a delay.**

The board wants a recommendation with a clear financial case in 8 weeks (kickoff-notes.md). The economic buyer — the CFO — will veto any case without a credible downside (cfo-interview.md). We therefore lead with the stress case: the realistic downside is a margin swing to **−3%** over 3 years, driven by patient-volume shift and coordination cost overrun (market-scan.md). A two-site pilot converts that modelled risk into observed data before any network-wide capital is committed, giving the CFO the downside protection she requires while giving the COO the "learn by doing" momentum he wants.

**One open risk to close before Thursday:** we have not confirmed the CFO will accept a pilot as *sufficient* downside protection (tension.md; hypothesis #3, confidence: low). Pressure-test this with her directly before the readout.

## 1. Downside / Sensitivity Case (lead here)
The CFO's decision rule is explicit and non-negotiable: *"If there's no sensitivity analysis, the answer is no"* and *"I've killed three business cases in two years — every one because the downside wasn't credible"* (cfo-interview.md).

- **Realistic downside: margin swing to −3% over 3 years**, driven by patient-volume shift and care-coordination cost overrun (market-scan.md).
- **Most common failure mode sector-wide: under-modelling patient-volume shifts** (market-scan.md) — the exact risk the CFO is probing when she says *"my job is to ask what happens in the year it isn't [smooth]."*
- **Blind spot to flag honestly:** the COO's feasibility narrative is light on downside quantification (coo-interview.md; tension.md). The −3% case must stand on its own evidence, not inherit operational optimism.

## 2. Base Case
- **+2–4% margin over 3 years**, consistent with peer networks (market-scan.md).
- Holds **only if patient-volume shift stays within the modelled band** (initial-hypotheses.md #1, confidence: medium).
- Boards decide in the spread between base and downside (market-scan.md) — so the base case is presented as the top of a range, never as a single number (which the CFO rejects outright — cfo-interview.md).

## 3. Upside / Feasibility
- The COO reports strong operational readiness: *"Clinically we're ready. Care coordination is already 80% of the way there"* (coo-interview.md).
- Payers are actively pushing outcome-based contracts amid margin pressure — the status quo is not risk-free (kickoff-notes.md).

## 4. Why a Pilot Is the Bridge
- **De-risks for finance:** replaces modelled downside assumptions with observed data. Peer evidence: *"Networks that piloted before full commitment retained margin better in year 1"* (market-scan.md).
- **Unlocks operations:** delivers the COO's preferred path — *"I'd rather pilot in two sites and learn"* (coo-interview.md).
- **Resolves the central tension** (ops ambition vs. financial discipline) rather than papering over it (tension.md).
- Frame explicitly as a **de-risking tool, not a delay** — the COO's fear is losing momentum (*"if we wait for a perfect forecast we'll never move"*).
